Complete Preparation Guide for JEE Main, JEE Advanced and NEET(UG) 2021



Eligibility Criteria 2021

JEE-Main <Click here>
  1. Number of Attempts in JEE Main:
    • The candidates get 3 attempts for JEE Main exam and 2 attempts for JEE Advanced.
    • In order to get admission in the states of Madhya Pradesh, Nagaland, Gujarat and Odisha institutions, the number of attempts and age limit will be similar to the previous practices of their respective state-level examinations.

  2. Nationality:
    • The candidate must be a Citizen of India.
    • Those who belong to the category of Non-Residential Indian (NRI) or Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) or Persons of Indian Origin (PIO) can also apply for JEE Main 2021, providing the category certificates at all stages of JEE Main.

  3. Number of Subjects Required for the Qualifying Examination (Class XII):
    • For Paper 1 (B.Tech), the candidates must have studied Physics and Mathematics as the compulsory subjects in Class 12th, and Chemistry/ Biotechnology can be optional subjects.
    • For Paper 2A & 2B, the candidates must have studied Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics as the compulsory subjects in Class 12th.

  4. Marks Required in Class XII (Qualifying Examination):
    • There are no minimum marks required in class XII for writing the JEE Main Exam.
    • For the year 2021, 75% in CLass 12th is not mandatory for admission in NITs, IIITs, GFTIs and SPAs.

  5. Date of Birth Criteria:
    • For General and OBC candidates whose birth date falls on or after 1st October, 1994 will be eligible to take JEE Main 2021.
    • However, in the case of the reserve category the upper age limit is relaxed by 5 years, i.e. people under the categories of Scheduled Caste (SC) and Scheduled Tribe (ST), as well as Persons with Disabilities (PwD) who are born on or after 1st October 1989, will be eligible.
    • The birth date given in the Secondary Education Board/University Certificate will be considered.

  6. Year of Appearance in Qualifying Examination (Class XII):
    • Only those who have cleared their Class XII examination or any equivalent qualification examination 2019 or 2020 or 2021 will be eligible to apply for JEE Main 2021.
    • The candidates who are appearing in class XII exam in 2021 or any equivalent qualifying exam can also apply for JEE Main 2021 exam.
    • The candidates who passed Class 12th in 2018 or before are not eligible to appear for JEE Main.

  7. Aadhar Card Criteria:
    8. Candidates appearing for JEE Main 2021 examination need not compulsorily mention details of the Aadhar card while filling up the application form. A notice issued by NTA has asked candidates to register any identification proof that can comprise of Passport, Bank Account Number, Ration Card Number, any valid Government ID or Aadhar Card.The form filling process for JEE Main has started and the candidates can now start filling it by entering details of valid and approved documents.
JEE Advanced <Click here>
  1. Performance in JEE Main 2021:
    2. Candidates who fall among the top 2,50,000 rankers in JEE Main 2021 across all categories are eligible to appear for JEE Advanced. Out of this number, the percentage of candidates eligible for admissions under different categories are as follows:
    • General category candidates (from the common merit list) - 46.5%
    • General EWS - 4%
    • OBC-NCL candidates - 27%
    • SC candidates - 15%
    • ST candidates - 7.5%
    • PWD candidates - Horizontal reservation of 5% in all the above categories

  2. Age limit:
    • General or OBC-NCL category candidates born on or after October 1, 1996, can apply for the exam.
    • However, the age limit for SC, ST or PwD category candidates has been relaxed. SC, ST or PwD candidates born on or after October 1, 1991, will be eligible for the exam.

  3. Number of attempts:
    4. A candidate can attempt JEE Advanced for a maximum of two times in two consecutive years

  4. Appearance in Class 12 (or equivalent) examination:
    • Candidates can appear for this engineering entrance exam if they have appeared for their Class 12 or equivalent exam for the first time in 2020 or 2021.
    • Candidates must have secured a minimum of 75% aggregate in Class 12 (or equivalent) board examination. The aggregate marks for SC, ST and PWD marks should be at least 65%
    • Candidates should be within category-wise top 20 percentile of successful candidates in their respective Class 12 (or equivalent) board examination.

  5. Earlier admission at IITs/ISM:
    • A candidate should NOT have been admitted in an IIT irrespective of whether or not he/she continued in the programme OR accepted an IIT seat by reporting at a reporting centre in the past. Candidates whose admission at IITs was cancelled after joining any IIT are also not eligible to appear in JEE Advanced 2021.
    • Candidates who have been admitted to a preparatory course in any of the IITs for the first time in 2020 can appear in JEE Advanced 2021.
    • The candidates who paid seat acceptance fee in 2020 but (i) did not report at any reporting centre OR, (ii) withdrew before the last round of seat allotment, OR, (iii) had their seat cancelled (for whatever reason) before the last round of seat allotment for IITs, during the joint seat allocation in 2020 are eligible to appear in JEE Advanced 2021.
NEET <Click here>
  1. Qualifying Exam for NEET 2021:
    • Candidates must have passed 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology and English as core subjects from a recognized board.
    • Class 12 or equivalent appearing aspirants are also eligible to apply for NEET.

  2. Age Requirement:
    • Candidates must have completed 17 years as on December 31, 2021.
    • The decision on the upper age limit is pending, so, all candidates are provisionally eligible to apply for NEET 2021.

  3. Qualifying Marks:
    4. The class 12 percentage required for NEET 2021 exam is separate for each category and can be listed as under. NEET eligibility marks in class 12 to be considered are aggregate marks only for PCB subjects.
    • UR - 50%
    • OBC/SC/ST - 40%
    • PWD - 45%

  4. Maximum Attempts:
    5. There are no restrictions on the maximum NEET attempt limit 2021.

  5. Nationality:
    6. Indian Nationals, NRIs, OCIs, PIOs & Foreign Nationals aspirants are eligible to apply.

How to apply?

JEE Main <Click here>
  1. Visit the NTA/JEE Main website.
  2. Use the JEE Main login credentials to fill the details in the application form.
  3. Upload the photograph, signature and Class 12 mark sheet.
  4. Verify all the details carefully.
  5. Application fee payment.
  6. Printout of the JEE Main confirmation page.
  7. Keep the confirmation page of JEE Main 2021 safe for reference.
JEE Advanced <Click here>
  1. The first step is to register for JEE Advanced 2021. The link to register for the exam will be provided here immediately after the same is released by the authorities.
  2. The aspirants need to upload the scanned certificates in the required format.
  3. The third step is to pay the JEE Advanced registration fee. The fee can be paid in both online and offline mode.
  4. Download the confirmation page.
NEET <Click here> As per the NEET 2021 latest news by NTA, the application form release date will be announced soon here. NEET application can be filled in five steps which include NEET registration 2021, filling of the application form, uploading of scanned images, payment of application fees, and a printout of the confirmation page.
